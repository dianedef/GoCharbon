# **L’impact de la gamification sur les CRM : un jeu qui transforme les règles**

### **Introduction**

**La transformation digitale a bouleversé le paysage des relations client. Les entreprises doivent constamment innover pour rester compétitives et garder leurs clients engagés. Une solution émergente qui a captivé l’attention des spécialistes du marketing et des gestionnaires de la relation client (CRM) est la gamification. Cette approche utilise des éléments de jeu pour rendre les interactions client plus ludiques et intéressantes. Dans cet article, nous explorerons l’impact de la gamification sur les CRM et son potentiel pour améliorer l’expérience client et stimuler la fidélité.**

### **La gamification en CRM : Qu’est-ce que c’est ?**

**La gamification est l’intégration de mécanismes de jeu dans des contextes non ludiques, tels que les processus commerciaux ou les interactions client. Dans le domaine des CRM, cela signifie rendre les activités de gestion de la relation client plus divertissantes et motivantes en y incorporant des éléments de jeu. Il s’agit d’appliquer des principes tels que les récompenses, les défis, les classements et les badges pour rendre l’engagement client plus attrayant et inciter à des comportements spécifiques.**

### **Les avantages de la gamification en CRM**

**La gamification offre de nombreux avantages aux entreprises qui cherchent à améliorer leurs CRM. Voici quelques-uns des principaux bénéfices :**

### **1. Amélioration de l’engagement client**

**Grâce à la gamification, les interactions client deviennent plus amusantes et impliquantes. Les clients sont incités à participer activement aux activités du CRM, ce qui les encourage à s’engager davantage avec la marque. Cela peut se traduire par une plus grande rétention des clients et une augmentation de leur fidélité.**

### **2. Motivation des employés**

**La gamification ne profite pas seulement aux clients, mais aussi aux employés chargés de la gestion des CRM. En intégrant des éléments de jeu dans leur travail quotidien, les employés sont stimulés et sont plus susceptibles d’atteindre leurs objectifs plus rapidement. Cela peut également améliorer leur satisfaction professionnelle et réduire le taux de rotation du personnel.**

### **3. Collecte de données précieuses**

**La gamification en CRM permet de collecter des données précieuses sur les clients. Les activités de jeu peuvent fournir des informations sur les préférences, les comportements et les besoins des clients, ce qui peut être utilisé pour personnaliser davantage les offres et les communications. Cette connaissance approfondie des clients permet aux entreprises de mieux cibler leurs efforts marketing et d’offrir une expérience client plus pertinente.**

### **Les exemples de gamification en CRM**

**La gamification peut être appliquée de différentes manières dans les CRM. Voici quelques exemples concrets de son utilisation :**

### **1. Programmes de fidélité**

**Les programmes de fidélité sont un exemple classique de gamification en CRM. En offrant des récompenses et des avantages spéciaux aux clients fidèles, les entreprises les encouragent à s’engager davantage et à rester fidèles à la marque. Les points de fidélité, les niveaux de statut et les récompenses spéciales sont autant de mécanismes de jeu utilisés pour stimuler la fidélité des clients.**

### **2. Badges et classements**

**Les badges et les classements sont des éléments de jeu populaires utilisés dans les CRM. Les clients peuvent gagner des badges en accomplissant des actions spécifiques, ce qui leur procure un sentiment d’accomplissement. Les classements permettent aux clients de se comparer aux autres et de se challenger pour atteindre des niveaux plus élevés. Cette compétition amicale peut-être très motivante pour les clients.**

### **3. Quêtes et défis**

**La mise en place de quêtes et de défis dans les CRM est une autre forme de gamification. Les clients sont encouragés à réaliser certaines actions ou à remplir desobjectifs spécifiques pour accéder à des récompenses ou débloquer de nouvelles fonctionnalités. Cela les incite à rester actifs et engagés avec la marque.**

### **Conclusion**

**La gamification a le potentiel de transformer la manière dont les entreprises gèrent leurs relations clients. En intégrant des éléments de jeu dans les CRM, les entreprises peuvent améliorer l’engagement des clients, motiver leurs employés et collecter des données précieuses. La gamification en CRM est un outil puissant pour renforcer l’expérience client et stimuler la fidélité. En adoptant cette approche innovante, les entreprises peuvent se démarquer de la concurrence et créer un avantage concurrentiel significatif.**

### **FAQ**

### **Q: La gamification en CRM convient-elle à tous les types d’entreprises ?**

**R: Oui, la gamification en CRM peut être adaptée à différents types d’entreprises, quelle que soit leur taille ou leur secteur d’activité. Les principes de base de la gamification, tels que les récompenses et les défis, peuvent être appliqués de manière flexible pour répondre aux besoins spécifiques de chaque entreprise.**

### **Q: Comment mesurer l’efficacité de la gamification en CRM ?**

**R: L’efficacité de la gamification en CRM peut être mesurée à l’aide de différents indicateurs, tels que le taux de participation des clients, la rétention des clients, le chiffre d’affaires généré par les clients fidèles, et la satisfaction des employés. En évaluant ces métriques, les entreprises peuvent évaluer l’impact de la gamification sur leurs résultats.**

### **Q: La gamification en CRM est-elle une tendance durable ?**

**R: Oui, la gamification en CRM est une tendance durable qui continuera à évoluer avec les avancées technologiques. À mesure que de plus en plus d’entreprises réalisent les avantages de la gamification, de nouvelles méthodes et de nouvelles applications seront développées pour améliorer encore les interactions client et les performances commerciales.**

## Qu'est-ce qu'un Serious Game?

Un Serious Game, qui se traduit par "jeu sérieux" en français, est un type de jeu vidéo à dimension pédagogique. Il s'agit d'une activité ludique qui vise à transmettre des savoir-faire, des connaissances et des compétences à son public. L'objectif principal de ce type de jeu vidéo est de concilier le divertissement avec l'acquisition de compétences réelles. Il stimule les utilisateurs tout en les faisant travailler et apprendre de manière moins formelle, obtenant ainsi d'excellents résultats. Cette approche s'inscrit dans la gamification, qui intègre les jeux et le divertissement dans des contextes sérieux où ils ne sont généralement pas présents.

## L'Intérêt du Serious Game

Le public cible des Serious Games est très diversifié, englobant des personnes de différents âges et profils, qu'il s'agisse d'étudiants, de professionnels, d'individus en reconversion professionnelle ou de personnes en recherche d'emploi. Les Serious Games sont particulièrement efficaces pour sensibiliser le public à des sujets importants de manière informelle et divertissante. Ils renforcent ainsi l'impact du message transmis.

## Avantages des Serious Games

Les Serious Games offrent de nombreux avantages. Ils permettent aux joueurs de développer des compétences professionnelles spécifiques, comme la programmation web, la maîtrise des outils bureautiques ou encore des mises en situation pour les professionnels des ressources humaines ou de la vente. De plus, organiser des Serious Games au sein d'une équipe ou d'une entreprise peut renforcer la cohésion autour d'un thème d'apprentissage spécifique, offrant ainsi une alternative intéressante aux activités de team building traditionnelles.

# Gamification Marketing: Literally Everything You Need to Know!

Gamification marketing is on the rise.

And that’s great news for all businesses.

[Implementing gamified content](https://xperiencify.com/gamification-design/) into your [digital marketing strategy](https://xperiencify.com/how-to-create-a-digital-marketing-strategy/) could be the game-changer you need.

While you may think that adding games to your [marketing strategy](https://xperiencify.com/inbound-marketing-strategy/) sounds like child’s play, it will help your [brand cut through the noise](https://xperiencify.com/brand-awareness/), stand out and avoid mediocrity.

You’ll drive engagement and promote your brand while creating an enjoyable experience for your site visitors.

Which will ultimately boost your conversion rates.

We’ll discuss what gamification marketing is, how it can benefit your business and best practices.

Let’s dive in.

## What is Gamification?

[Gamification](https://xperiencify.com/gamification/) infuses game mechanics such as competition, rankings, and scoring systems, into non-game contexts.

It’s all about taking fun, interactive and compelling elements from games and applying them to day-to-day activities.

Why would you do that?

[Gamification](https://yukaichou.com/education-gamification/how-to-gamify-an-online-course-with-octalysis/) aims to make a product, service or training less yawn-worthy, more engaging, and, ultimately, more attractive.

So, how does gamification apply to digital marketing?

## What is Gamification in Digital Marketing?

By adding games to your [digital marketing strategy](https://xperiencify.com/digital-marketing/), you’re gamifying an activity to encourage participation and [digital engagement](https://xperiencify.com/digital-engagement/), which helps to promote a new product or service.

Simultaneously, you’re offering attainable rewards to your site visitors, such as points, leader boards, and trophies.

Gamifiying your content helps boost sales, create more active collaborations, improve ROI, strengthen [customer loyalty](https://www.dixa.com/blog/how-the-service-recovery-paradox-can-help-boost-post-pandemic-customer-loyalty/), and drive customer satisfaction.

Some of the biggest brands in the world have yielded incredible success with gamification marketing.

For example, take a look at these gamification marketing statistics:

- The “Ford Escape Route” game resulted in [over $8 million in vehicle sales](https://www.datasciencecentral.com/profiles/blogs/gamification-realizing-elusive-engagement) for Ford Motors, a 600% increase in [Facebook likes](https://xperiencify.com/facebook-marketing/), and over 100 million Twitter impressions.
- Domino’s “Pizza Hero” [increased sales by 30%](https://www.datasciencecentral.com/profiles/blogs/gamification-realizing-elusive-engagement) simply by allowing customers to create their own pizza!

You can see the benefits experienced by Ford and Domino’s. But what benefits can you expect?

## The Benefits of Gamification in Marketing

Why is gamification important in marketing?

Since its rise in popularity in 2010, gamification has proven to be a kick-butt marketing strategy that’s here to stay.

Over time, however, consumers have become savvier to the growing number of digital marketing strategies.

As a result, they know when they are being targeted and can end up feeling ambivalent toward sales tactics.

However, gamification taps into peoples’ [intrinsic motivation](https://xperiencify.com/intrinsic-vs-extrinsic-motivation/) while providing valuable extrinsic rewards—both tangible and intangible.

Plus, [89% of employees](https://www.talentlms.com/blog/gamification-survey-results/) believe gamification in the workplace [boosts productivity](https://xperiencify.com/employee-engagement/). This is why [gamification in corporate training](https://xperiencify.com/gamification-in-corporate-training/) works so well for employee onboarding, [compliance training](https://xperiencify.com/compliance-training/), and skill training.

Let’s move on to 14 **gamification marketing benefits** and discuss why they’re so effective in digital marketing.

### 1. Increased User Engagement


We’re living in an age where people turn to a screen for entertainment in their downtime.

Visitors are more likely to become—and remain—faithful customers if a company incites fun and excitement.

Gamification works because people love to feel a sense of achievement.

And win stuff.

### 2. Increased Conversions

Users are more likely to respond to your call to action (CTA) than non-gamified marketing methods.

Attaching rewards to your gamification efforts will encourage your audience to take steps that will boost your conversion rates.

In fact, around [70% of marketers](https://www.demandgenreport.com/resources/infographics/the-state-of-interactive-content-marketing-statistics-and-trends/) believe [interactive content](https://xperiencify.com/content-marketing-tools/) successfully converts visitors to customers.

### 3. Increased Brand Awareness and Loyalty

Gamification leads visitors to think more about your brand, which helps to influence buyer behavior.

Most users don’t associate games with marketing, so they become unconsciously receptive to strategically placed branding.

Gamification encourages shoppers to purchase a marketed brand when they’re shopping due to brand recall.

Take it from almost [90% of marketers](https://www.demandgenreport.com/resources/infographics/the-state-of-interactive-content-marketing-statistics-and-trends/) who find [interactive content](https://xperiencify.com/content-creator/) helps their brand [stand out from their competitors](https://xperiencify.com/how-to-conduct-a-competitive-analysis/).

### 4. Multiplatform Functionality

It comes as no surprise that most people access the internet, [social media](https://xperiencify.com/social-media-marketing-funnel/), and apps via smartphones and tablets, as well as desktops and laptops.

[Gamification](https://xperiencify.com/gamification-apps/) can easily be adapted for all platforms, which helps you reach a wider audience.

### 5. Consumer Data Collection

Consumer data is a valuable asset for businesses.

But, gathering it isn’t always easy. Plus, you must ensure you collect it ethically.

The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) states how data should be handled by organizations collecting data.

But gamification has made the intentional capture of personal data possible and is an excellent way to gain consensual, GDPR compliant data.

If visitors see a chance to win a prize or reward, they’re more willing to hand over [their personal information](https://xperiencify.com/how-to-create-a-buyer-persona/).

You can then use this data as part of your marketing strategy.

### 6. Easy on the Pocketbook

Gamification is an easy-to-implement and cost-effective strategy that can propel your business forward.

Some [gamification elements](https://xperiencify.com/gamification-elements/) may look fancy, but they’re not as expensive and time-consuming as they appear.

### 7. Gets Through AdBlockers


No one likes pesky pop-ups—whether you’re searching for information or browsing shopping sites. This is why [27% of people use ad blockers](https://www.statista.com/statistics/804008/ad-blocking-reach-usage-us/) in the US.

But, ad blockers limit a company’s reach, wasting money in the process.

The good news is, ad blockers don’t apply to gamification. As a result, games easily bypass users who have the extension installed.

### 8. Entices Consumers

Often, consumer behavior is based on emotion rather than rationale.

For example, site visitors may ignore a clickable banner advertising 20% off. But they could feel drawn to a 20% discount offer after passing three levels in an interactive game.

Users want to feel that they have “earned” a discount, which leads them to value the reward more than a discount given for no reason.

### 9. Educates Consumers

Gamification is a fun way to inform users of the benefits of your products or services.

For example, Swiss milk company, Emmi AG, launched a digital campaign to promote their new low sugar range. Emmi created three games in the form of a jackpot machine.

Users learned loads of facts and answered market research style questions as they played, which allowed Emmi to educate consumers while gathering valuable consumer data.

### 10. Reaches a Wide Audience

As of 2020, mobile gaming accounted for [51% of the global gamification market](https://www.visualcapitalist.com/how-big-is-the-global-mobile-gaming-industry/)—totaling $85 billion in revenue.

Games appeal to both kids and the young at heart. As a result, they’re the best way to reach a broad demographic for any business looking to attract consumers of all ages.

### 11. Creates Positive Emotions

The [psychology behind gamified marketing](https://www.galacticfed.com/blog/marketing-psychology-six-principles-that-ll-help-you-attract-more-customers) is quite simple.

Users play a game and earn a reward.

The accomplishment helps them feel a sense of achievement, which leads to a [positive association with your brand](https://xperiencify.com/build-brand-loyalty/).

Win-win!

### 12. Great for Social Media

[Social media channels](https://xperiencify.com/b2b-social-media-marketing-strategy/) such as Twitter, Facebook, [Instagram](https://xperiencify.com/instagram-analytics/), and LinkedIn are the best platforms for [cultivating a social buzz](https://xperiencify.com/monetize-social-media/) around your [digital marketing campaign](https://xperiencify.com/digital-marketing-statistics/).

Adding human-centric design elements, such as an option for [social media shares](https://xperiencify.com/social-media-analytics/), is a fantastic way to drive [user engagement](https://xperiencify.com/user-engagement/) and fosters a community of competition and play.

We, humans, are social creatures and crave adventure, amusement, and achievement.

## Best Practices for Gamification Implementation


To reap the rewards, you’ve got to get it right.

### 1. KISS (Keep It Simple, Silly)

Games should be fun.

Duh.

So you don’t want to turn people away by complicating a fun experience.

Keep your gamification simple so that your audience can easily understand how to play—and win.

### 2. Know Your Target Audience

[Your target audience](https://xperiencify.com/target-audience/) must always be your priority.

What do your customers like?

What will attract them to your site?

Consider these questions, and don’t just rush off to implement the latest and greatest gamification techniques.

It is essential you first ensure your audience can relate to your gamified content.

That’s where research will help you decide the best gamification technique to target the right audience that’s receptive and ready to interact with your brand.

### 3. Reward Your Players

Would you turn down the chance to win rewards just by playing a simple game?

Well, neither would your visitors!

Begin by rolling out small prizes. Then, if you want to get your users addicted to your game—and we know you do—pique their curiosity and get ‘em hooked!

For example, if you have a spin-to-win game on your site, give users three spins of the wheel for a chance to win.

After each spin, they can either redeem their reward or try again. But, players will lose any bonus won if they choose to spin again.

### 4. Encourage Friend Challenges

Encourage the innate human need to compete, win, and be social.

Allow your audience to invite friends to play by adding [Twitter](https://xperiencify.com/twitter-statistics/), Facebook, and Instagram share buttons to your gamified content.

This will not only make it more competitive and interactive, but you’re also expanding your reach!

### 5. Promote the Heck Out of It

Promotion is key to any successful marketing endeavor.

You want to reach a broad audience with your gamified content, so you must promote it.

Where are you most likely to reach your target audience?

[Social media](https://xperiencify.com/social-media-management/)?

With [1.9 billion daily users](https://www.statista.com/statistics/346167/facebook-global-dau/), Facebook should be at the top of your list.

Select other platforms such as Twitter, Instagram, and [Linkedin](https://xperiencify.com/linkedin-statistics/) based on your audience’s behavior and preferences.

[Email marketing](https://xperiencify.com/email-marketing/) is a great way to [invite your subscribers](https://xperiencify.com/email-marketing-software/) to participate in your new game.

## Awesome Marketing Gamification Examples

Here are some gamification examples to show you how you can apply gamified marketing to your business.

### 1. ”Spin-to-Win” Wheels


Spin-to-win discount wheels give visitors a chance to earn coupons for your store simply by handing over their contact information.

Spin-to-win stands out, and you can include elements such as discounts, special offers, or free shipping.

Let’s say a visitor spins the wheel and wins a 25% discount. As a result, they may feel inclined to complete their purchasing journey in your store.

This gamification style can also significantly reduce the rate of cart abandonment while generating new leads for your business.

### 2. Loyalty Reward Programs


A [loyalty program](https://xperiencify.com/loyalty-programs/) incentivizes purchases by allowing customers to accumulate a specified number of points before converting them into an offer. The incentive may be freebies or discounts on future purchases.

One of the most [successful gamification examples](https://xperiencify.com/gamification-examples/) is Starbucks.

Gone are the old punch cards. Today, Starbucks encourages regular purchases using the “Starbucks Dash.”

Members are rewarded for visiting a store multiple times over a specific timeframe. They then receive loyalty program points the more often they call into a store.

A [mobile app](https://xperiencify.com/mobile-app-gamification/) keeps track of visits, encouraging members to move the progress bar towards earning a special reward.

### 3. Digital Badges
