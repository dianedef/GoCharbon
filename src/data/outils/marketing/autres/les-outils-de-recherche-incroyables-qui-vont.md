Transforme Ma Vie (TMV) a pour mission de démocratiser l'accès à des informations fiables et

scientifiquement validées dans les domaines de la psychologie, de la santé et de la gestion de la

violence. Notre objectif est d'accompagner chaque personne dans son parcours de transformation

personnelle jusqu'à l'épanouissement en société et l'accomplissement de ses rêves. Nous suivons une

méthodologie rigoureuse pour sélectionner et valider les informations que nous publions.

  

## Nos Engagements

  

### 💡 Accessibilité

  

Nous nous efforçons de rendre des informations complexes accessibles à tous, sans en sacrifier la

précision ou la profondeur.

  

### 🔄 Mise À Jour Continue

  

Nos contenus sont régulièrement mis à jour pour refléter les dernières avancées scientifiques et les

meilleures pratiques.

  

### ⚖️ Transparence

  

Nous citons systématiquement nos sources et reconnaissons les limites de nos connaissances actuelles

quand elles existent.

  

### 🎯 Échanges Et Partage

  

Nous organisons des lives trimestriels gratuits et ouverts à tous, où vous pouvez échanger

directement avec des professionnels du bien-être sur des thématiques spécifiques. Ces sessions

interactives sont l'occasion de poser vos questions et d'approfondir différents aspects du

développement personnel avec des experts reconnus.

  

## Notre Méthodologie

  

### 1. Sélection Rigoureuse Des Sources

  

Nous nous appuyons exclusivement sur :

  

- Des publications scientifiques évaluées par les pairs

- Des ouvrages de référence reconnus

- Des experts qualifiés dans leurs domaines

- Des institutions officielles et reconnues

  

### 2. Processus De Validation

  

Chaque article passe par un processus rigoureux :

  

1. Recherche approfondie des sources primaires

2. Vérification croisée des informations

3. Mise à jour régulière des contenus

4. Consultation d'experts si nécessaire

sciences de la vie, contenant plus de 8 millions d'articles.

# **Juan Camilo Mesa on Elicit and Afforai: A Deep Dive into Scientific Literature Tools**

## Introduction

In a recent post, Juan Camilo Mesa, a renowned Nutritionist-Dietitian and Microbiologist, shared insights into his use of two powerful tools, Elicit and Afforai, for scientific literature searches. This article provides a comprehensive overview of his experiences, highlighting the advantages, disadvantages, pricing, and the tool he ultimately chose.

## **Elicit: Unraveling the Power of Scientific Inquiry**

### *Overview*

Juan Camilo Mesa praises Elicit as one of the most useful tools in the current landscape of artificial intelligence, specifically designed for scientific research.

### *Functionality*

- Elicit focuses solely on scientific research and can generate summaries with evidence, sources, and answers to research questions.
- Users can access the papers used by the AI, complète with summaries and direct links for further exploration.
- The tool allows for filtering evidence based on study types, catering to specific research needs such as randomized controlled trials (RCTs), meta-analyses, and systematic reviews.

### *Drawbacks*

- Operated by ChatGPT, Elicit's responses may occasionally sound peculiar. However, Juan emphasizes that the focus should be on the returned articles rather than the robot's opinion.
- Formulating precise questions is crucial, not only due to credit consumption but also to avoid missing the opportunity to resolve a query.

### *Pricing*

- Initially offered for free, Elicit has transitioned to a paid model.
- A basic plan with minimal credits is available for free, while a monthly subscription of $10 offers a more extensive credit allowance.
- Juan expresses the worthiness of the $10 monthly fee, emphasizing the remarkable capabilities of the tool.

## **Afforai: Broadening Horizons Beyond Scientific Journals**

### *Introduction*

Juan introduces Afforai as a newcomer to the market, distinct from Elicit in that it searches the entire internet, not just scientific articles.

### *Functionality*

- Afforai allows users to train their own bots with personal information and engage in conversations with documents.
- Similar to Elicit, Juan initiates inquiries without the necessity for a research-focused question, and the tool returns answers while citing quality sources.

### *Drawbacks*

- In comparison to Elicit, Afforai lacks filters, requiring users to inquire specifically about certain study types. However, this feature is expected to be added soon.
- Despite this limitation, Juan notes that Afforai excels at its current capabilities.

### *Unique Features*

- Afforai comes with a default library of prompts, offering useful instructions for interacting with bots and PDF documents.
- Users, familiar with ChatGPT, can create and save their own prompts, enhancing the interaction with the tool.

## **Conclusion: Making the Choice**

In his exploration of Elicit and Afforai, Juan Camilo Mesa provides a valuable comparison of two tools catering to distinct aspects of scientific literature searches. Each tool has its strengths and limitations, and the choice ultimately depends on individual preferences and specific research needs.

## **Future Outlook**

As technology continues to evolve, the dynamic nature of tools like Elicit and Afforai suggests ongoing improvements and additional features. Researchers, scientists, and enthusiasts alike can look forward to more sophisticated tools that streamline the process of accessing and understanding scientific literature.

In conclusion, Juan's detailed account serves as a guide for those navigating the landscape of AI-driven scientific research tools, shedding light on the nuanced considerations that come with each choice.
