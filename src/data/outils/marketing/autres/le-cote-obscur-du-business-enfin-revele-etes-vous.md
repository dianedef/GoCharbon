In the court system, judges often look for precedent from “like” cases. In this situation, you can look into Dropbox. Early on Dropbox’s website was [trydropbox.com](http://trydropbox.com/?fbclid=IwAR1csi3YCPpiG6c3SxT7Panj7u3vw3BeCCTaOInTIbdTLYst6FkBzYe-Ze0) because Dropbox was taken. They offered the owner equity and cash for the domain but he refused and launched a copy cat website on [Dropbox.com.](https://dropbox.com/?fbclid=IwAR331ZHGS3DU0pdZRqhIMNGA4XtfxDEt5up9ctfn_DpeeYXiCZhsDemRco0) As they worked through the court system the owner ended up settling and selling the domain to Dropbox for 500k. Judges looking into the case would have likely ruled in favor of [trydropbox.com](http://trydropbox.com/?fbclid=IwAR2tBAoRus3b5OUwMlGyTrtY4-b4XK-D9VRBqpux6O2xoCb-oJpmvyhV76U)’s favor because the copy cat website was in violation of many laws (this was pre DMCA)
