	Frank Liberto revolutionized the ballpark concession industry by popularizing nachos as a stadium snack through his family business in San Antonio, Texas, creating a lasting nachos monopoly in sports and entertainment venues.
	- Ballpark nachos have become a staple at sporting events, with millions of orders sold annually thanks to innovations from Frank Liberto.
	- Frank Liberto, after taking over his family food business, Ricos, introduced nachos to sports stadiums, beginning with the Texas Rangers in the 1970s.
	- Liberto's idea was to offer a quick-serve version of nachos by using pre-melted cheese, which could be served efficiently at concession stands.
	- The iconic nachos at stadiums consist of tortilla chips, a special cheese sauce, and jalapeños.
	- Ignacio "Nacho" Anaya originally invented nachos in the 1940s in Piedras Negras, Mexico.
	- Liberto's entrepreneurial skills led to collaborations with companies like La Costeña and Dean Foods to perfect the ingredients for mass production.
	- The nachos proved to be highly profitable, not impacting sales of other concession items but creating new revenue streams for venues.
	- Frank Liberto’s marketing prowess led to partnerships with establishments beyond ballparks, including movie theaters, driven by a successful promotional campaign with Coca-Cola.
	- Ricos adjusted their business strategy over the years to compete amidst larger conglomerates in the snack food market.
	- Today, Ricos products are sold in numerous US stadiums and movie theaters, and they have expanded their product sales into grocery stores.
