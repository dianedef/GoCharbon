Context is KEY

Not because it's magical, but because it narrows the "space" (indeed, AI operates under "vectorial" spaces) in which AI works and improves results.

With Triplo AI, when you add //context//, what you're doing is pushing the content (or context SmartPrompt) you selected to feed the Model to better serve you.

Context, in fact //context// always comes first and it's FULLY considered by AI when generating the content. Be careful, a 10.000 context, costs you…. well… 10.000 tokens.

You begin your prompts with it because you want AI to "know" and consider it before anything else.

But how is that different from a MIND?
(which is analogous to a pre-built knowledge base)

First, a Mind can be gigantic, and it certainly won't feed entirely into a prompt; it may hold up to 200,000 tokens (that's the equivalent of two full romance novels).

What Training does is search that Mind and feed your prompt with the content that best aligns with it as context. Something around 1.000 tokens that matches what you need.

Think of the Mind use as a "dynamic context" for your prompts. It's really effective since it's content you know and trust that will not only enrich your prompts but also narrow the aforementioned 'space,' hence yielding better results.

Great, if you're following along so far, let's turn all of this into something simpler that doesn't require you to write long prompts every time you need insights and content. 

Enter Custom SmartPrompts

We've built three types of them: Context, Normal, Inline.

They are nothing but predefined prompts and, trust me, they are VERY powerful and useful tools to save you time.

Context Custom SmartPrompts work just like the context I explained. Choose the content you always use (a webpage, content from a manual, a procedure, etc.), and it's always there, ready to "narrow the vectorial space" and enrich your prompts.

Normal Custom SmartPrompts are the most common and useful. They contain the "commands" you want the AI to follow when generating results.

If you recall the "Hidden Prompting Structure" I shared, you can make pretty much all of your regular prompts dynamic and will be able to use your Custom SmartPrompts with variables you choose when prompting (NOTE: variableS, plural).

Inline Custom SmartPrompts are meant to replace common commands you use in your prompts.

Think of phrases like "Be polite and professional while maintaining an approachable demeanor" (I use this often but not always, for example) or "The results MUST always be presented in bullet points and in American English."

You get the idea… Short commands you use often and don't want to type every time you prompt.

What's missing?

Instructions

This is the simplest part. Remember the analyst metaphor?

Imagine meeting him and telling him something like: "I am the director of XYZ. Whatever you generate, make it like this, this, and that. Never use jargons. Be precise and avoid hallucinating."

Once he understands who you are and what you expect of him, he will do his best to serve you (in the end, you rule it!).

The last one … //selection//

It's common for people to become confused by the nuances of Context and Selection. Despite months of searching for a clearer explanation, I've yet to find a better one than this: Context is what I want AI to KNOW before working the prompt. Selection is what I want it to process.

Take this example: It's irrelevant to me whether the AI understands that "Mozilla's annual report shows a significant increase in the CEO's salary while Firefox's market share continues to decline." I simply need it translated into French, hence the prompt: tofr //selection//

Nor do I expect the AI to analyze the pricing on a webpage I'm browsing. Instead, I require the AI to identify and list the product names and prices, so I prompt: "Please extract the product names and prices from: //selection//"

Remember, Selection is akin to the raw material for an artist — it's what the AI shapes and works with.

NOTE: know that in this context, Scraped content is analog to Selection.

1 - I though this was sort of public knowlege by now but looks like it's not. The "intelligence" on AI is actually not what we intend for "intelligence" itself. AI machines now are more of a very capable "memory" machine that is able to build "coherent" text out of it. It lacks FACTs and is not really capable of reasoning anything it says... Sounds and feels like "intelligent" but IT's NOT.

This is why on Triplo AI we avoided the "long form" generation or any sort of "batch" generation. I like metaphores. We see AI now as a "strong contractor" that needs guidance and supervision of a manager (YOU) to be sure he won't put everything to loose. YOU are the one who's capable to judge if AI returned you good/true results. AI is the one that carries the heavy load of building texts, emails, gather insights (sometimes false) etc.

I'll put in caps because it's important: AI is not INTELLIGENT. YOU are the intelligent side of this relation. You MUST verify what it's suggesting to you (always) and this is not a Triplo AI limitation. It's a general limitation of Artificial Intelligence machines at this point in time.
