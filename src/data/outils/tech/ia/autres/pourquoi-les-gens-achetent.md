**The four letter code to sell anything** _Consumers are torn between two opposing forces: neophilia, a curiosity about new things; and neophobia, a fear of anything too new. As a result, they gravitate to products that are bold, but instantly comprehensible. [Loewy](https://j0l1y7h.r.us-east-1.awstrack.me/L0/https:%2F%2Fwww.theatlantic.com%2Fmagazine%2Farchive%2F2017%2F01%2Fwhat-makes-things-cool%2F508772%2F/1/010001858c5908e4-2f2a07b4-abf0-41d7-bc8c-6498a4d068f8-000000/2dtkFpsswROGdtViJL_5JUXtIk8=303) called his grand theory “Most Advanced Yet Acceptable”—maya. He said to sell something surprising, make it familiar; and to sell something familiar, make it surprising._

_Simplify until it's obvious._
