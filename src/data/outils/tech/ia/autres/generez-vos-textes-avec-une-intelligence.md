## Actus

[Prompt Building & AI Enthusiasts | Facebook meta has released a new model with 175B parameters | Facebook](https://www.facebook.com/groups/533283571187905/posts/595225604993701/)

[Blog Google AI : Résumés générés automatiquement dans Google Docs](https://ai.googleblog.com/2022/03/auto-generated-summaries-in-google-docs.html)

Les résumés générés automatiquement ne seraient pas possibles sans les progrès considérables du ML pour la [compréhension du langage naturel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Natural-language_understanding) (NLU) et la [génération du langage naturel](https:// [en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Natural_language_generation](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Natural_language_generation)) (NLG) au cours des cinq dernières années, en particulier avec l'introduction de [Transformer](https://arxiv.org/abs/1706.03762) et [Pegasus]([](https://ai/)[https://ai](https://ai) .googleblog.com/2020/06/pegasus-state-of-art-model-for.html).

[Résumé de texte abstrait](https://ai.googleblog.com/2016/08/text-summarization-with-tensorflow.html), qui combine les tâches individuellement difficiles de compréhension et de génération de langage de document long, a été un long- problème permanent dans la recherche NLU et NLG. Une méthode populaire pour combiner NLU et NLG consiste à entraîner un modèle ML à l'aide de [sequence-to-sequence](https://arxiv.org/abs/1409.3215) apprentissage, où les entrées sont les mots du document et les sorties sont le résumé mots. Un réseau de neurones apprend ensuite à mapper les jetons d'entrée sur les jetons de sortie. [Applications précoces](https://arxiv.org/abs/1602.06023) du paradigme séquence à séquence utilisé [réseaux de neurones récurrents](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Recurrent_neural_network) (RNN) pour à la fois l'encodeur et le décodeur.

L'introduction de Transformers a fourni une alternative prometteuse aux RNN, car les Transformers utilisent [l'auto-attention](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Attention_(machine_learning)) pour fournir une meilleure modélisation des longues dépendances d'entrée et de sortie, ce qui est essentiel. dans le résumé des documents. Pourtant, ces modèles nécessitent de grandes quantités de données étiquetées manuellement pour s'entraîner suffisamment, de sorte que l'avènement de Transformers à lui seul n'a pas suffi à faire progresser de manière significative l'état de l'art en matière de résumé de documents.

La combinaison de Transformers avec une pré-formation auto-supervisée (par exemple [BERT](https://arxiv.org/abs/1810.04805), [GPT]([https://openai.com/blog/better-language-models](https://openai.com/blog/better-language-models) /), [T5](https://arxiv.org/abs/1910.10683)) a conduit à une percée majeure dans de nombreuses tâches NLU pour lesquelles des données étiquetées limitées sont disponibles. Dans la pré-formation auto-supervisée, un modèle utilise de grandes quantités de texte non étiqueté pour apprendre la compréhension générale du langage et les capacités de génération. Ensuite, dans une étape ultérieure de réglage fin, le modèle apprend à appliquer ces capacités à une tâche spécifique, telle que la synthèse ou la réponse aux questions.

Le travail de Pegasus a poussé cette idée un peu plus loin, en introduisant un objectif de pré-formation adapté à la synthèse abstraite. Dans la pré-formation Pegasus, également appelée [Gap Sentence Prediction](https://arxiv.org/abs/1912.08777) (GSP), les phrases complètes d'articles de presse et de documents Web sans étiquette sont masquées à partir de l'entrée et le modèle est nécessaire pour les reconstruire, en fonction des phrases non masquées restantes. En particulier, GSP tente de masquer les phrases considérées comme essentielles au document par différentes heuristiques. L'intuition est de rendre la pré-formation aussi proche que possible de la tâche de synthèse. [Pegasus a obtenu des résultats de pointe](https://ai.googleblog.com/2020/06/pegasus-state-of-art-model-for.html) sur un ensemble varié d'ensembles de données de synthèse. Cependant, un certain nombre de défis subsistaient pour appliquer cette avancée de la recherche à un produit.

[https://ci3.googleusercontent.com/proxy/mX0T9M6Qv1LPh3HMWCx3QP0qddmehS4jvvkWMwTFxG_9rQsU8Rk-dbyWeCGyt3-y9CXif0doaKkk_zQwts2pTpMD99FY9tVJ0WeVGCfPOCqPD8x7HMiIxRpcsc4F-KD8cNrr=s0-d-e1-ft#https://ph-files.imgix.net/979e4e9b-9b46-4feb-9297-c516e7d14560.jpeg?auto=format](https://ci3.googleusercontent.com/proxy/mX0T9M6Qv1LPh3HMWCx3QP0qddmehS4jvvkWMwTFxG_9rQsU8Rk-dbyWeCGyt3-y9CXif0doaKkk_zQwts2pTpMD99FY9tVJ0WeVGCfPOCqPD8x7HMiIxRpcsc4F-KD8cNrr=s0-d-e1-ft#https://ph-files.imgix.net/979e4e9b-9b46-4feb-9297-c516e7d14560.jpeg?auto=format)

This year has been particularly interesting for folks interested in AI, especially when it comes to image generators. After OpenAI’s [release of DALL-E 2](https://s-links.producthunt.com/lnk/EAAAAz3zdJIAAcgxd24AAK5QDBkAAAAIijYAAAAAAAYklQBjF1IPKbP-9rsGRO-7MZIQGi87wQAF1QU/7/2Keafzsg-RavRT42oY3gFw/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cucHJvZHVjdGh1bnQuY29tL25ld3NsZXR0ZXIvMTA4ODUtYXJ0aWZpY2lhbC1nZW5lcmFsLWludGVsbGlnZW5jZT91dG1fY2FtcGFpZ249MTQ0MTVfMjAyMi0wOS0wNSZ1dG1fbWVkaXVtPW5ld3NsZXR0ZXImdXRtX3NvdXJjZT1EYWlseStEaWdlc3QmdXRtX3Rlcm09ZWRpdG9yaWFs) a few months ago, we’ve continued to see similar products spring up.

An [interesting article](https://s-links.producthunt.com/lnk/EAAAAz3zdJIAAcgxd24AAK5QDBkAAAAIijYAAAAAAAYklQBjF1IPKbP-9rsGRO-7MZIQGi87wQAF1QU/9/vCB3rbbsxWkEca_alZye8g/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cud2FzaGluZ3RvbnBvc3QuY29tL3RlY2hub2xvZ3kvMjAyMi8wOS8wMi9taWRqb3VybmV5LWFydGlmaWNpYWwtaW50ZWxsaWdlbmNlLXN0YXRlLWZhaXItY29sb3JhZG8v) from Washington Post tells the story of how maker Jason Allen, who runs a tabletop fantasy games company, submitted an artwork generated by Midjourney (another AI tool) and won a fine-arts competition. It’s hard to tell how this will play out in the long run and whether technology like DALL-E and Stable Diffusion will completely remove the need for creative jobs or will simply be used as a tool to enhance creativity.

Safety concerns around the ethics of AI could be what delays the process though. And just like ATMs replaced bank tellers, change is inevitable as technology advances.

What if AI can paint itself a different picture? A couple of new launches present a very charming use of AI that’s definitely not the same vibes as the unnerving humanoid Haley Joe Osment portrayed on the silver screen.

That means stories can feature your child (or nephew, etc.) or their favorite character. These custom, easy-to-build stories can also conveniently be used to teach pertinent lessons to your child, like how to clean up their toys.

So, the overall consensus on AI writing software is that none are better than the other and they all suck?

I wouldn't say they "suck". Rather, some people seem to have too high expectations.

In my experience, an AI can (1) help unstick from writer's block, (2) save some time with less important tasks such as summarizing a text, and (3) help SEO efforts.

My expectation is to throw away up to 80% of what the AI generates, and almost fully rewrite the remaining 20%.

For shorter texts (ads, blog post titles, email titles) the quality of AI output might be much better.

For programming-related AI use, the success can be even higher, because we don't need to worry about originality and (mostly) style.

I've only seen one really inadequate AI tool (it was a PG LTD of some sort) that was trying to evaluate vocabulary, grammar and tone of expression. That one simply couldn't get British English.

Apart from that outlier, general-purpose AI textwriting tools require knowledge and "hand guiding".

The better AI tools will give you more advice and context about how to use the tools, and will further help you by giving you an ever growing list of 'recipes' (e.g. Nichesss).

Others will simply require that you spend anywhere between 10 and 50 hours to figure them out (Closers' Copy).

In any case, there is a steep learning curve, and a productivity multiplier at the top.

That multiplier is bigger as the required individual output is shorter. For a longer type of output (my articles average 3.5-5K words and require research) the overhead of trying to train the AI, provide context, go through multiple rewrites, etc. the benefit is minimal outside the convenience of the editor (e.g. the Scalenut rephrase functionality).

Beaudoin takes that concept and applies it to your reading feed in his new app, Waverly.

**How to customize a feed:** With [Waverly](https://s-links.producthunt.com/lnk/EAAAA5Ycij4AAcgxeawAAK5QDBkAAAAIijYAAAAAAAYklQBjmLcqlODDJ-3cSdGtlzmMnmyLZQAF1QU/12/wrNtVmHIwiYvAf6JhHZ2ag/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cucHJvZHVjdGh1bnQuY29tL3Bvc3RzL3dhdmVybHktMj91dG1fY2FtcGFpZ249MTcwMjJfMjAyMi0xMi0xMiZ1dG1fbWVkaXVtPW5ld3NsZXR0ZXImdXRtX3NvdXJjZT1EYWlseStEaWdlc3QmdXRtX3Rlcm09ZWRpdG9yaWFs), you can create your own “waves,” or feeds of articles related to a niche topic that interests you. Or you can follow waves created by others, like “Understanding Consciousness” or “Parenting for New Parents.” From the time you create a new Wave with a prompt (i.e. what you want to read), you’re training the AI to recognize what kind of content you’re interested in. Then you can continue refining the original prompt, or you can do other things that help train the AI, like save articles from the internet into your wave to read later.

**The tech:** [Waverly](https://s-links.producthunt.com/lnk/EAAAA5Ycij4AAcgxeawAAK5QDBkAAAAIijYAAAAAAAYklQBjmLcqlODDJ-3cSdGtlzmMnmyLZQAF1QU/13/R8F5rtojwxfUof6E6Q62BA/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cucHJvZHVjdGh1bnQuY29tL3Bvc3RzL3dhdmVybHktMj91dG1fY2FtcGFpZ249MTcwMjJfMjAyMi0xMi0xMiZ1dG1fbWVkaXVtPW5ld3NsZXR0ZXImdXRtX3NvdXJjZT1EYWlseStEaWdlc3QmdXRtX3Rlcm09ZWRpdG9yaWFs) uses prompt engineering to generate your feed. If you’ve tried ChatGPT or one of the aforementioned image products, you’ve taken part in this concept already. Your input (or prompt) contains instructions or examples that convince large language models (LLM) to produce the content you want. As with any of these products, you might not get [exactly what you want](https://s-links.producthunt.com/lnk/EAAAA5Ycij4AAcgxeawAAK5QDBkAAAAIijYAAAAAAAYklQBjmLcqlODDJ-3cSdGtlzmMnmyLZQAF1QU/14/heu1mycszaoEOt-z2exwsQ/aHR0cHM6Ly90d2l0dGVyLmNvbS9jaXVyYV92aWN0b3Ivc3RhdHVzLzE1ODE2MTM1NzM5NjAxNzk3MTI_bGFuZz1lbg) out of your first prompt so you might have to refine it, but since prompts are often only a few words or sentences, doing so is quite easy.
